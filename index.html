Research

Wood Gesellschaft Applied Physics Laboratory is a small private research institute which focuses on scalability in science and engineering through open source robotics and artificial intelligence.

  • Robotics. We are working on open source humanoid hands for prosthetics and robotics. We are currently interested in multiphysics based state estimation of deformable objects and affordable 3D printed contact sensors embedded in synthetic skin for model predictive control of humanoid robot manipulators. Human beings interact with our environment primarily through our hands, as our feet are primarily used to support us since we came down out of the trees. Duplicating the interactions of humans with their environment is in a large part imitating how our hands manipulate objects.
  • Artificial Intelligence. We study spatial reasoning for intelligent agents as well as question answering systems. Strong AI is important to scalability in science as it is to increasing the quality of human life in general as it will allow our potential work hours to scale roughly with the price of electricity instead of being constrained by the size of the available human workforce along with countless economic factors.

Applications

As a small private research institute, Wood Gesellschaft Applied Physics Laboratory is at a disadvantage in our search for funding from traditional sources due to competition from larger, more well-established research institutes with more broadly defined goals and effectively infinitely more man hours to throw at any particular task. Because of this handicap in our efforts to compete with more established institutions for funding, Wood Gesellschaft Applied Physics Laboratory has invested our precious time, money, and expertise in engineering and physics over the last several months to advancements in horticulture.

  • Vertical Horticulture.We are developing low cost vertical horticulture technology because money really does grow on trees in our largely glucose-based economy. Our vertical hydroponic gardening products allow the output of horticultural operations to scale in size by volume as opposed to area as with traditional gardening methods. We believe this technology will be all important in coming years to avoid any adverse affects of population pressure on food security. Many thanks to Larry Athey for his videos describing his method of constructing vertical towers for indoor hydroponics.
  • Agricultural Robotics and Automation.Our research in Robotics and Artificial Intelligence has many applications to the agriculture industry. The Blue Challenge from Naturipe is a perfect example of how important dexterous robotic manipulation is to scalability in agricultural sciences.
